Executive summary
03

The bottom line

Indeal today reads to buyers as a list of items — a hybrid catalog and lead‑generation tool — not yet a true B2B marketplace. The commercial operating system a procurement team needs (pricing logic, MOQ, negotiation, credit terms, multi‑item RFQs, role‑based teams, verified suppliers) is incomplete or missing.

What's there

A bilingual storefront, a 3‑step seller wizard with Wathq integration, and a working RFQ thread with file uploads.

What's missing

Negotiation, MOQ, tiered pricing, credit terms, role-based teams, multi-line RFQ, structured quotes — the core of B2B.

What's broken

Dead pages, blank search results, raw translation keys leaking, seller PII publicly exposed, and silent verification hangs.

Executive summary
04

Top three themes

Where the partner pain is concentrated.

The contract layer of B2B is missing

No tiered pricing, no MOQ, no negotiation, no credit terms, no PO/VAT handling, no role-based teams. Buyers cannot transact the way procurement teams actually buy.

Trust signals are absent and seller PII is exposed

Personal Gmail addresses and mobile numbers of 2,264 sellers are public — encouraging off-platform deals and undermining marketplace economics.

Quality bugs are visible to end-users

Raw translation keys leak to the buyer dashboard, search returns empty for products that exist, and the seller verification step hangs indefinitely on invalid input.

Cross‑cutting observation
05

It feels like a list of items, not a B2B marketplace

A flat catalog of SKUs in consumer‑style cards — not a procurement environment.

What buyers see

Image, name, "Price on Request" or a single retail‑looking price, Add to Cart. No MOQ, no lead time, no verified‑supplier signal.

The mental model

The journey is "shop", not "source". No project, no sourcing list, no quote comparison, no team review.

The mismatch

B2B vocabulary is on the surface; the underlying mechanics behave like a small e‑shop.

Cross‑cutting observation
06

Search & discovery do not work for procurement

Buyers can't reliably find products, and can't filter by what matters.

Two search systems

/search returns blank for valid queries. /products?q= works — but isn't what the header points to.

No B2B facets

No filters for industry, specs, supplier, MOQ, lead time, origin or certifications. No sort by price, MOQ or rating.

Empty featured categories

Categories like Polymers are surfaced on the homepage but resolve to 0 products.

Cross‑cutting observation
07

RFQ creation & product information are too thin

Two of the most B2B‑defining surfaces are the weakest in the product today.

RFQ

No multi‑item RFQ

RFQs are created from a single product page, one SKU at a time. Real procurement is multi‑line.

  • • No RFQ basket or multi‑supplier bundling
  • • No line‑item quantities or target price
  • • No required delivery date or incoterms
  • • No document upload at creation
PDP

Product info isn't B2B‑grade

PDPs carry name, image, short description and a price — buyers can't evaluate suitability without leaving the platform.

  • • No structured specs, HS code or origin
  • • No datasheets, SDS or COA
  • • No MOQ, pack/case, lead time or incoterms
  • • No verified supplier profile or samples

Onboarding
& Registration

Two distinct journeys — buyer (light, e-commerce-style) and seller (3-step wizard with CR validation). Both have meaningful gaps in a B2B context.

1 · Onboarding · Buyer
09

Buyer registration is treated as B2C High impact

The buyer profile collects only Full Name, Email, and Phone. There is no concept of a company, role, industry, tax ID, or team.

Profile gaps

  • No company name, industry, size, or VAT/CR field
  • No "role within company" (procurement / finance / plant manager)
  • No way to invite teammates or operate as a corporate account
  • Phone is optional — should be required and verified for B2B

Address form gaps

  • No postal code, no Saudi-city dropdown — State and City are free text
  • No address type (billing / shipping / warehouse)
  • No building / floor / delivery instructions
  • No tax / VAT registration number for invoicing
1 · Onboarding · Seller
10

Seller wizard — good structure, fragile execution Critical

The 3-step flow (CR Verification → Business Info → Shop Details) is conceptually right. Execution has hard issues.

Hard failures

  • Hangs indefinitely on unknown CR — no timeout, no error toast, no recovery
  • Regex requires CR to start with "7" — blocks valid Riyadh CRs (1010…)
  • No "what happens next" or approval SLA is communicated to the seller

Soft failures

  • Wathq integration is client-side; console shows repeated Skipping button addition
  • Wizard state is lost on page refresh
  • Duplicate validation toasts displayed for the same error
1 · Onboarding · Verification
11

Verification & approval are opaque High impact

2,264 vendors are listed — most with placeholder logos and no badge that distinguishes verified, certified, or new sellers. Buyers have no signal of trust.

Missing trust signals

  • No tiered verification badges (CR-verified, Saudi Made, ISO-certified)
  • No public KYC indicator on seller cards or product pages
  • "Started from" date shown but no other tenure or response metric

Data quality

  • Duplicate sellers visible (e.g. ARTIC × 2 with identical contact info)
  • 2,264 seller emails are personal Gmail addresses, not business
  • Personal mobile numbers exposed publicly on every seller card

Section 4 of 7

RFQ Journey
Post-Submission

Once an RFQ is sent, the buyer lands on a thread-style view. It supports messaging and attachments — but it is a chat, not a quote-management system.

4 · RFQ post-submission
19

A chat thread, not a quote system

The post-submission experience supports messaging and document attachments — a real strength. But it is missing the structured "quote" object that turns a conversation into a transaction.

What works Working

/customer/quote-requests/35
  • Thread-style messaging between buyer and seller
  • File attachments (PDF, DOC, XLS, PNG, ZIP) up to 10 MB
  • RFQ list with status pill and unread counter
  • Submission timestamp and request ID visible

What's missing Critical

  • No structured "quote" object with price + lead time + validity + terms
  • No accept / reject / counter-offer mechanism
  • No "convert quote to order" button
  • Status is binary ("Pending") — no Quoted / Counter-offered / Accepted / Lost states
  • No SLA / overdue indicator on stale RFQs
  • Untranslated string plugins/fob-request-quote::request-quote.vendor visible
5 · Post-purchase journey
20

Pre-fulfillment surfaces only

Because no real order could be placed end-to-end (Cash on Delivery only, no live fulfillment), this section is bounded to confirmation and pre-shipment surfaces.

Limitations of this audit

No public sandbox with a test order. The buyer account has no historical orders. The only payment method is Cash on Delivery, which produces no online receipt to inspect. Findings are limited to cart, checkout, and the empty Orders dashboard.

Findings on the visible portion High

  • Tax line shows 0.00 — Saudi 15% VAT not auto-computed
  • No order-status pipeline (Placed → Accepted → Packed → Shipped → Delivered)
  • No downloadable VAT invoice / proforma after order placement
  • "Shipping fees not included" — no landed-cost estimate before checkout
  • Empty Orders state offers no "import a previous PO" entry point

How we close these gaps

A B2B platform layer purpose-built for the gaps surfaced in this audit — paired with AI assistants that compress the work for buyers and sellers alike.

AI Sourcing Assistant

Buyers describe needs in plain language; the assistant returns matched SKUs across sellers with normalized specs.

Smart RFQ from BOMs

Auto-extract line items from spreadsheets and PDFs; fan out one structured RFQ to multiple sellers.

Quote Intelligence

Side-by-side comparison and outlier flags on price, lead time, MOQ and terms — built into every quote thread.

Seller Copilot

Auto-fill product specs, draft responses, translate AR ↔ EN, and suggest pricing — sellers reply in minutes, not days.

B2B-grade Design

Purpose-built UI for procurement — specs, MOQ, tiers, batches, certificates — not a consumer storefront in disguise.

ERP & Systems Integration

Clean APIs and connectors for SAP, Oracle, Microsoft Dynamics, Odoo and the buyer's accounting and PO systems.

Configurable, not Fragmented

Tenant-level customization (branding, workflows, taxonomies) without breaking the shared B2B contract layer.

Trust Layer

Tiered verification, masked PII, platform-routed messaging and an exportable audit trail for procurement compliance.

Marketplace Ops

Search & taxonomy hygiene, RTL & localization fixes, performance budgets, and observability so silent failures stop being silent.

Recommendations · P1
28

P1 · Critical — ship first

The five moves that most directly address what partners are complaining about today.

01

Ship a structured Quote object with negotiation primitives

Replace the free-text RFQ thread with a quote artefact (price, lead time, MOQ, validity, payment terms, attached docs) that can be sent, countered, accepted, expired, and converted into an order.

P1
02

Add MOQ + tiered pricing as first-class fields on every product

Require sellers to enter MOQ and at minimum 3 price tiers (1-49, 50-499, 500+). Surface them in listings, product pages, and cart validation.

P1
03

Stop exposing seller PII publicly

Mask personal mobile numbers and replace personal Gmail addresses with platform-routed inboxes. Keep transactions on-platform — protect revenue.

P1
04

Introduce credit terms, VAT invoicing, and PO support at checkout

Add Bank Transfer, Mada, STC Pay, Net-30/60, a PO number field, and automatic 15% VAT computation with a downloadable tax invoice.

P1
05

Fix seller onboarding hangs and CR validation

10s timeout with retry on Wathq, widen the regex to all valid Saudi CR prefixes (1010, 2050, 4030, 5800, 7…), persist wizard state across refreshes.

P1
Recommendations · P2
29

P2 · High — remove visible friction

06

Unify search and fix dead pages

Retire the broken /search route. Add a "0 results" empty state with category suggestions. Repair /sellers, /polymers-hub, /customer/addresses (plural).

P2
07

Make filters category-aware and clean the tag taxonomy

Build per-category filter schemas (Food: weight, packaging, halal, shelf life; Construction: dimensions, grade, standard). Editorial process for tags.

P2
08

Upgrade buyer profile into a Company Account with roles

Members, roles (Admin, Procurement, Approver, Viewer), spending limits, and approval workflows for orders above a threshold.

P2
09

Rewrite product detail pages to a B2B specification template

Mandatory: spec table, ≥3 images, datasheet, HS code, certifications. Block listings below a minimum data-completeness score.

P2
10

Add multi-line RFQ creation

Buyers compose an RFQ with multiple SKUs across one or more sellers in a single submission. Reuses the new Quote object from R-01.

P2
11

Introduce verified-seller tiers

Three visible badges: CR Verified, KYC + Saudi Made, Indeal Trusted. Filter by badge in browse.

P2

12

Fix Arabic RTL hero banner and complete the localization pass

Performance budget and observability

P3

The bottom line

Indeal has the visual identity and bilingual foundation of a credible Saudi B2B marketplace. What it is missing is the commercial operating system beneath the surface — quotes, MOQ, tiers, terms, teams, and trust. Closing that gap is what will turn dissatisfied partners into repeat ones.
