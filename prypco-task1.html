PRYPCOPrinciples

Before adding anything new, I asked:
what's actually broken?

And this was my prioritization approach.

Based on that

1
Fix what's broken first.
Emirates ID gate, T&Cs error, empty shelf post-KYC. No new feature fixes a broken funnel. Sprint 1 starts with bug fixes, not builds.
2
Measure before you move.
Drop-off rates today are estimates, not data. Instrumentation ships before everything else. Without a baseline, every improvement is invisible.
3
Unblock the most users fastest.
Registration, KYC roadmap, smart empty state. One sprint each, high impact, low effort. Not glamorous but they remove the friction that matters.
4
AI features need to be built right.
The wizard, OCR, and retargeting go in Sprint 2. They touch the most critical moments. Rushing them risks new bugs in the exact flow we're fixing.
5
Everything earns its place.
No nice-to-haves. Every initiative fixes a known drop-off point or drives a conversion event. If capacity forces cuts, medium-impact items go first.
