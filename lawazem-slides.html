SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • All-in-one procurement SaaS
  • Free 1-day delivery nationwide
  • Multi-level approvals & analytics
  • Experienced founding team (20+ yrs)
  • Deloitte Fast 50 #1 ranking

Weaknesses

  • 10–15% product markup vs retail
  • Limited public reviews / social proof
  • App-dependent (downtime risk)
  • Minimum spend thresholds

Opportunities

  • Expand into government e-procurement
  • AI-driven demand forecasting
  • Regional expansion beyond KSA
  • Cross-sell events & hospitality services

Threats

  • Jarir / Amazon expanding B2B offerings
  • Price-sensitive SME market
  • Startup replication risk
  • Platform dependency concerns

Recommendation
9

Recommendation

Lawazem offers fair value — primarily for the clients it was designed for. The 10–15% premium is justified by integrated procurement controls, analytics and free 1-day delivery for high-volume enterprise buyers.

01

Prioritize enterprise deals

Focus sales on mid-to-large accounts (banks, government, hospital chains) where workflow automation saves more than the markup costs.

02

Address price perception

Produce ROI calculators showing total cost savings vs. decentralised sourcing to justify the Cost+ markup during sales cycles.

03

Invest in social proof

Publish case studies and seek independent reviews (Trustpilot / G2) to build credibility with conservative procurement teams.
