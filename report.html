Executive summary

Indeal today functions as a hybrid catalog and lead‑generation tool more than a true B2B marketplace. To buyers it reads as a list of items — close to a consumer storefront — rather than a procurement platform. Core B2B mechanics (pricing logic, MOQ, tiered discounts, structured negotiation, credit terms, multi‑line RFQs, role‑based teams, and verified seller trust signals) are either missing, partially implemented, or technically broken. Partner dissatisfaction is a rational response to those gaps.

Top three themes Critical

  • The platform is missing the contract layer of B2B. No tiered pricing, no MOQ, no negotiation, no credit terms, no PO/VAT handling, no role-based teams. Buyers cannot transact the way procurement teams actually buy.
  • Trust signals are absent and seller PII is exposed. Personal Gmail addresses and mobile numbers of 2,264 sellers are public — encouraging off-platform deals and undermining marketplace economics.
  • Quality bugs are visible to end-users. Raw translation keys (plugins/fob-request-quote::request-quote.vendor) leak to the buyer dashboard, search returns empty for products that exist, and the seller verification step hangs indefinitely on invalid input.

What works today Strengths

  • A mobile-first bilingual storefront with a clear EN/AR toggle.
  • RFQ post-submission view supports messaging + file attachments (PDF, DOCX, XLS, etc., up to 10MB) — a strong foundation for procurement workflows.
  • Three-step seller onboarding wizard with CR (Commercial Registration) integration via Wathq is the right architectural choice for the Saudi market.
  • A "Saudi Made" badge framework exists and aligns the marketplace with Vision 2030 positioning.
Cross‑cutting observation

It feels like a list of items, not a B2B marketplace

The site reads as a flat catalog of SKUs in consumer‑style cards. The signals B2B buyers expect — supplier verification, MOQ, lead time, incoterms, price breaks, payment terms, samples, specs, documents — are missing or buried.

Search & discovery

Buyers can't reliably find products, and can't filter by what matters.

  • • /search returns blank for valid SKUs
  • • No industry, spec, supplier, MOQ or lead‑time filters
  • • Featured categories resolve to 0 products
RFQ creation

RFQ flow is single‑SKU; real procurement is multi‑line.

  • • No multi‑item RFQ, no RFQ basket
  • • No target price, delivery date or incoterms
  • • No documents at the point of creation
Product information

PDPs lack the data needed to make a procurement decision.

  • • No specs, datasheets, SDS or COA
  • • No MOQ, pack/case, lead time or incoterms
  • • No verified supplier profile or samples

1 · Onboarding & registration

Two distinct journeys exist — buyer (light, e-commerce-style) and seller (3-step wizard with CR validation). Both have meaningful gaps for a B2B context.

Buyer flow
Seller flow
Verification

Buyer registration is treated as B2C High impact

Source · /customer/edit-account, /customer/address/create

The buyer profile collects only Full Name, Email and Phone. There is no concept of a company, role, industry, tax ID, or team. The address form is generic shipping data with free-text State and City — no validation against Saudi cities, no postal code, no building/floor, no separate billing vs delivery vs warehouse address types, and no VAT/Tax registration number.

  • No company name, industry, company size, or VAT/CR field on the buyer profile.
  • No "role within company" (procurement, finance, plant manager) — needed for routing and permissions.
  • Address form lacks postal code, address type, and Saudi-city dropdown; State and City are free text.
  • Phone is optional; for B2B follow-up this should be required and verified.
  • No way to invite teammates or operate as a corporate account.

Seller wizard — good structure, fragile execution Critical

Source · /customer/become-vendor

The 3-step flow (CR Verification → Business Info → Shop Details) is conceptually correct. In testing, however, step 1 has hard issues: the regex requires CR numbers to start with "7", which excludes valid Riyadh-region CRs starting with 1010. When a non-existent CR is entered, the "Check & Next" button enters a perpetual Loading… state with no timeout, no error toast, and no recovery path — the user is silently stranded.

  • Hangs indefinitely on invalid/unknown CR numbers (no timeout, no error message).
  • Overly restrictive regex: "must start with 7" blocks legitimate CRs (Riyadh = 1010xxxxxx).
  • Wathq integration runs client-side; console shows WathqApi: Skipping button addition repeatedly — fragile coupling.
  • No progress save: refreshing during the wizard loses all entered data.
  • Duplicate error toasts displayed for the same validation failure (UI bug).
  • No "what happens next" / SLA after submission ("approval within X days").

Verification & approval are opaque High impact

Once a seller submits, there is no public audit trail of how Indeal validates them. On the seller listing page, 2,264 vendors appear — most with placeholder logos, generic Gmail addresses, and no badge that distinguishes verified, certified, or new sellers. Buyers have no signal of trust.

  • No tiered verification badges (e.g., CR-verified, Wathq-verified, Saudi Made-certified, ISO-certified).
  • No public KYC indicator on seller cards or product pages.
  • Seller "Started from" date is shown on product pages but no other tenure / responsiveness metric.
  • Duplicate sellers visible in catalog ("ARTIC" listed twice with identical contact info) — no dedup pipeline.

2 · Ordering experience

Two transaction journeys coexist — Request a Quote and direct Add to Cart — but their availability is inconsistent and the choice is hidden from the user.

Request a Quote (RFQ) Limited

Source · /products/baharat-kabsa-naem (modal)

The RFQ modal is product-scoped: one product, one quantity, optional message. It cannot represent a real procurement scenario — multiple SKUs, attached specifications, target prices, required delivery date, or Incoterms.

  • Single-line only — cannot bundle multiple SKUs into one RFQ.
  • No file attachment in the initial form (uploads only become available after submission).
  • No fields for target price, required-by date, delivery location, payment-terms preference, or unit of measure.
  • Quantity defaults to "1" — not a B2B-realistic anchor.
  • Phone & company are optional, undermining downstream sales qualification.

Direct purchase (Add to Cart) Inconsistent

Source · /products/florida-orange-pineapple-juice-16-ltr → /checkout

A standard B2C flow exists for products with a published price. It works mechanically, but the checkout is missing every B2B essential: PO number, VAT/tax invoice details, credit terms, freight quotation, multi-warehouse split. Tax shows 0.00 SAR even though Saudi VAT is 15 %.

  • Only "Cash on Delivery" payment method offered. No bank transfer, Mada, STC Pay, corporate card, or credit terms.
  • VAT (15 %) not auto-calculated; tax line shows 0.00.
  • No PO number or tax-invoice fields at checkout.
  • Shipping options limited to "Local Pickup" or "Flat Rate 20 SAR" — no real freight quote.
  • No clear rule explaining why a product shows "Add to Cart" vs "Request a Quote" — same category contains both.

3 · Product listings

The catalog renders quickly but product data is shallow, pricing is mostly hidden, filters are not category-aware, and search is partially broken.

Pricing visibility is the single biggest catalog problem Critical

The vast majority of products surfaced in browsing display "Price on Request." For a buyer trying to compare suppliers, the catalog is effectively unusable as a price-discovery surface. Where prices do exist (e.g. Florida Juice at 19.95 SAR), they are unit prices with no MOQ, no tiered breaks, no carton/case context, and no implied VAT.

  • Most products: "Price on Request" with no indicative range.
  • Where shown, price is a single number — no quantity-break tiers.
  • No MOQ shown anywhere on listing or detail pages.
  • No unit-of-measure context (per kg / per carton / per ton / per piece).

Product data is too thin for B2B decisions High impact

Source · /products/baharat-kabsa-naem and others

The example "Kabsa Spice Blend" page has a one-sentence description and three generic bullets ("Balanced aromatic mix", "True Gulf taste", "Easy to use"). There are no datasheets, certifications, country of origin, HS code, packaging spec, shelf life, batch info, or available pack sizes. A single image is the norm.

  • No structured specifications table (origin, weight, dimensions, packaging, shelf life, HS code, halal/ISO certifications).
  • No technical documents / datasheets attached to listings.
  • Most products have only one image; no gallery, video, or 360°.
  • Truncated product titles in grid view ("Sun-Dried Bla…") with no tooltip.

Filters & search are inconsistent and partially broken Critical

  • Two parallel search systems: the popup at top works (/products?q=…), but the dedicated /search?q=… URL returns blank — even for known products like "kabsa".
  • No empty-state or "0 results" message on the broken /search route — page just renders blank.
  • Filters are not category-aware: a "Size" filter offering mattress dimensions (90-CM (900×190×220MM)) appears on a page of dried fruit and spices.
  • Tag filter mixes Arabic and unrelated English tags ("rgb", "led", "KTP", "gaming") — suggesting tag taxonomy is unmanaged.
  • No autocomplete suggestions; no "did you mean"; no synonym handling (Arabic/English).
  • No bulk-purchase filters: cannot filter by "available in bulk", MOQ range, lead-time, or country of origin.

Bulk purchasing is not a first-class concept Critical

The catalog never asks the buyer "how much do you need?" before showing options. There is no quantity-aware sort, no tiered pricing display, no carton/pallet calculator, no consolidated cart-by-seller, and no comparison feature. A procurement officer evaluating 10 SKUs across 5 sellers must currently click each product individually.

4 · RFQ journey (post-submission)

Once an RFQ is sent, the buyer lands on a thread-style view. It supports messaging and attachments — but it is a chat, not a quote-management system.

What is in place Working

Source · /customer/quote-requests/35
  • RFQ list page with status pill (Pending) and unread counter.
  • Thread-style messaging between buyer and seller.
  • File attachment supported: PDF, DOC, DOCX, XLS, XLSX, JPG, PNG, TXT, ZIP, RAR — up to 10 MB.
  • Submission timestamp and request ID visible.

What is missing Critical

  • No structured "quote" object: seller cannot send a price + lead-time + validity + payment-terms response that the buyer can accept / reject / counter.
  • No counter-offer mechanism — negotiation happens in free text only, with no audit trail of price changes.
  • No "convert quote to order" button — the buyer must manually start a separate purchase.
  • Status is binary ("Pending") — no granular states (Quoted, Counter-offered, Accepted, Expired, Lost).
  • No expected-response SLA or overdue indicator visible to the buyer.
  • Untranslated string plugins/fob-request-quote::request-quote.vendor appears as a label — quality bug visible to end-users.
  • No way to share an RFQ with a colleague (no team / collaborator concept).
  • No notifications shown — unclear if email/SMS is sent on seller reply.

Evidence — leaked translation key

plugins/fob-request-quote::request-quote.vendor → Saudi Top Plastic Factory

Appears on /customer/quote-requests and on the request detail page. Should render as "Vendor" or "Seller". Visible in production today.

5 · Order journey (pre-fulfillment)

Because no real order could be placed end-to-end (Cash-on-Delivery only, no live fulfillment available for evaluation), this section is bounded to the confirmation and pre-shipment surfaces.

Limitations of this evaluation

The audit could not validate post-payment fulfillment, carrier integration, delivery tracking, returns processing, or invoice generation, because: (a) the only available payment method is Cash on Delivery, which produces no online payment receipt; (b) there is no public sandbox with a test order; and (c) the test buyer account has no historical orders to inspect. Findings below are limited to what is observable in cart, checkout, and the empty Orders dashboard.

Findings on the visible portion High impact

  • Checkout summary shows "Tax: 0.00" — Saudi 15 % VAT not auto-computed; non-compliant for B2B tax invoices.
  • "Shipping fees not included" disclaimer appears in cart total — buyer cannot estimate landed cost before checkout.
  • No order-confirmation preview is shown before payment; buyer has no way to check, e.g., expected delivery window.
  • Empty Orders state is friendly but offers no jumping-off point for procurement (e.g., "import a previous PO").
  • No order-status pipeline visible (Placed → Accepted by seller → Packed → Shipped → Delivered).
  • No downloadable VAT invoice / proforma after order placement.

6 · Critical B2B capabilities — gap matrix

Eight capabilities every serious B2B marketplace ships. Today, Indeal has zero of them in production-quality form.

Price negotiation
Missing
Negotiation happens in free-text chat with no structured offer/counter-offer, no price history, no validity, no accept button. Buyers cannot demonstrate audit trail to finance.
RFQ document upload
Partial
Attachments are supported on the post-submission thread (10 MB, common formats) but not on the initial RFQ form, where specs typically need to be shared first.
Multi-line RFQ
Missing
RFQ is one product per request. A procurement officer with a 30-line BOM must create 30 separate RFQs and message 30 separate threads.
MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)
Missing
No MOQ field on listings, no MOQ validation in cart, no MOQ filter in browse. Buyers learn the seller cannot fulfill only after they ask.
Tiered / volume pricing
Missing
Where a price is shown, it is a single number. No 1-99 / 100-499 / 500+ ladder. The fundamental B2B pricing primitive does not exist.
Credit terms & flexible payment
Missing
Cash on Delivery is the only option in checkout. No Net 30/60/90, no bank transfer, no Mada, no STC Pay, no purchase-order workflow, no credit-limit logic.
Buyer–seller messaging & audit trail
Weak
A messaging thread exists per RFQ but there is no global inbox, no read receipts, no pinned attachments, no exportable audit log for compliance, and no notification preferences.
Role-based team access
Missing
No company account, no users, no roles. Procurement, finance approver, plant manager, admin — all collapse into a single login. Cannot enforce spend limits or approval flows.

8 · Prioritized recommendations

A focused 90-day plan ordered by partner-satisfaction impact, not by engineering cost. P1 items unlock revenue mechanics that cannot be substituted for; P2 items remove visible friction; P3 items mature the platform.

01

Ship a structured Quote object with negotiation primitives

Replace the free-text RFQ thread with a quote artefact — price, lead time, MOQ, validity, payment terms, attached docs — that can be sent, countered, accepted, expired, and converted into an order. Add status states (Pending → Quoted → Counter-offered → Accepted → Lost / Expired). This single change addresses the four most-cited B2B gaps and creates a defensible audit trail.

P1 · Critical
02

Add MOQ + tiered pricing as first-class fields on every product

Require sellers to enter MOQ and at minimum 3 price tiers (e.g. 1-49, 50-499, 500+). Surface them on listing cards, the product page, and in cart/quantity validation. Without these, the marketplace cannot truthfully claim to support B2B.

P1 · Critical
03

Stop exposing seller PII publicly

Mask personal mobile numbers and replace personal Gmail addresses with platform-routed inboxes. This protects sellers, keeps transactions on-platform (revenue!), and removes a class of GDPR-style risk. Add an explicit "Contact via Indeal" CTA instead.

P1 · Critical
04

Introduce credit terms, VAT invoicing, and PO support at checkout

Add Bank Transfer, Mada, STC Pay, Net-30/60 (initially gated by manual seller approval), a PO number field, and automatic 15 % VAT computation with a downloadable tax invoice. Without these, finance teams cannot use the platform.

P1 · Critical
05

Fix seller onboarding hangs and CR validation

Add a 10-second timeout with a clear error and retry on the Wathq lookup; widen the regex to accept all valid Saudi CR prefixes (1010, 2050, 4030, 5800, 7…); persist wizard state across refreshes; deduplicate toast errors.

P1 · Critical
06

Unify search and fix dead pages

Retire the broken /search route in favour of /products?q=. Add a proper "0 results" empty state with category suggestions and synonym fallback. Repair /sellers, /polymers-hub, /customer/addresses (plural) — they appear in navigation but 404 today, eroding credibility.

P2 · High
07

Make filters category-aware and clean the tag taxonomy

Mattress dimensions should not appear on a spice page. Build a per-category filter schema (Food: weight, packaging, halal cert, shelf life; Construction: dimensions, grade, standard) and an editorial process for tags. This is the lowest-cost lift to noticeably improve discovery.

P2 · High
08

Upgrade the buyer profile into a Company Account with roles

Promote "Buyer" to "Company" with members, roles (Admin, Procurement, Approver, Viewer), spending limits, and approval workflows for orders above a threshold. This is the unlock for selling into mid-market and enterprise.

P2 · High
09

Rewrite product detail pages to a B2B specification template

Mandatory: structured spec table (origin, weight, packaging, MOQ, lead time, certifications), at least 3 images, attached datasheet, HS code. Optional: video, batch info, sustainability scoring. Block listings that fall below a minimum data-completeness score.

P2 · High
10

Add multi-line RFQ creation

Allow buyers to compose an RFQ with multiple SKUs across one or more sellers in a single submission, with line-level quantity, target price, and required-by date. Reuses the new Quote object from R-01.

P2 · High
11

Introduce verified-seller tiers

Three visible badges: CR Verified, KYC Verified + Saudi Made, Indeal Trusted (with track record). Filter by badge in browse. Trust signals are the single biggest brake on first orders from new buyers.

P2 · High
12

Fix Arabic RTL hero banner & complete the localization pass

The homepage hero text is visibly broken in RTL. Run a localization audit on every customer-facing string, fixing leaked keys (plugins/fob-request-quote::*) and missing translations.

P3 · Mature
13

Performance budget and observability

Set a 2.5 s LCP / 3 s TTI budget; pre-compress images on the catalog (most are oversized); add real-user monitoring and an error reporter so that silent failures (Wathq hang, /search blank) become visible to the product team without a buyer complaint.

P3 · Mature

The bottom line

Indeal has the visual identity and bilingual foundation of a credible Saudi B2B marketplace. What it is missing is the commercial operating system beneath the surface — quotes, MOQ, tiers, terms, teams, and trust. Closing that gap is what will turn dissatisfied partners into repeat ones.
